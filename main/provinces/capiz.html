NAME ORIGIN:The province of Capiz in Panay Island, Western Visayas, was named after the kapis shell, which was abundant in the area.


POPULATION: 835,098
Young dependents (aged 14 and below) account for 29.71% of the total. The economically active population (those aged 15 to 64 who form the actual or potential workforce) comprises 63.72%of the total. Finally, senior citizens (65 and over) represent the remaining 6.57%.


GOVERNMENT: The Capiz government is led by Governor Fredenil Hernaez Castro and operates through a Provincial Board, overseeing one city (Roxas) and 16 municipalities, structured with two congressional districts, handling provincial affairs for this province.
