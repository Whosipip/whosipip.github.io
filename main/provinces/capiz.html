Region VI
Capiz Province

Capital: Roxas City Island: Panay Island Languages: Capiznon, Hiligaynon Geography Coastal plains Rich fishing grounds Economy Fishing and aquaculture Seafood processing Agriculture Known as Seafood Capital of the Philippines Famous Tourist Spots Baybay Beach Olotayan Island Pan-ay Church Festival Sinadya sa Halaran Festival

NAME ORIGIN:The province of Capiz in Panay Island, Western Visayas, was named after the kapis shell, which was abundant in the area.

POPULATION: 835,098 Young dependents (aged 14 and below) account for 29.71% of the total. The economically active population (those aged 15 to 64 who form the actual or potential workforce) comprises 63.72% of the total. Finally, senior citizens (65 and over) represent the remaining 6.57%.

GOVERNMENT: The Capiz government is led by Governor Fredenil Hernaez Castro and operates through a Provincial Board, overseeing one city (Roxas) and 16 municipalities, structured with two congressional districts, handling provincial affairs for this province.

FAMOUS FOODS: 1. Talaba (Oysters) 2. Diwal (Angel Wings Clam) 3. Puyoy (Eel) 4. Crabs, Lobsters, and Mussels 5. Blue Marlin & Catfish
