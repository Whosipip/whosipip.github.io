NAME ORIGIN: The name Guimaras is believed to have two possible origins: one from a Portuguese mariner naming it after Guimaraez (the first capital of Portugal), and another from local folklore about two ill-fated lovers, Princess Guima and Aras.


POPULATION: Around 193,000
Young dependents (aged 14 and below) account for 29.33% of the total. The economically active population (those aged 15 to 64 who form the actual or potential workforce) comprises 64.08% of the total. Finally, senior citizens (65 and over) represent the remaining 6.58%.


GOVERNMENT:Guimaras has a provincial government under the Philippine system, led by a Governor (currently Lucille Nava) and a provincial legislature (Sangguniang Panlalawigan), overseeing its five municipalities, each with its own Mayor and council, functioning as a local government unit (LGU) for integrated coastal management, development, and public services.


FAMOUS FOODS:
1. Guimaras Mangoes
2. Mango Mango Pizza
3. Mango Bibingka


FAMOUS PRODUCT: Mangoes
Guimaras has earned fame for its mangoes, which many consider the sweetest in the world.
