Western Visayas
Region VI

Western Visayas, officially designated as Region VI, is an administrative region in the Philippines occupying the western section of the Visayas. The region comprises the islands of Panay and Guimaras. It consists of five provinces, namely: Aklan, Antique, Capiz, Iloilo, and Guimaras. The region also includes one highly urbanized city, Iloilo City, which is the largest city and serves as the region's capital.

The region is known for its rich agricultural and marine resources, producing major crops such as rice, sugarcane, corn, and various tropical fruits, including mangoes and bananas. Guimaras, in particular, is famous for its sweet mangoes. Western Visayas is a leading tourist destination, home to the world-renowned Boracay Island, and heritage landmarks of Iloilo. Its economy continues to grow through agriculture, tourism, manufacturing, and trade.

The Hiligaynon language, native to Iloilo City, is the region's lingua franca. The region is also dominated by native speakers of three Visayan languages: Kinaray-a, Aklanon, and Capiznon. The land area of the region is 12,750.63 sq. km(4,923.05 sq. mi), and with a population of 4,730,771 inhabitants.

HistoryRegions first came into existence on September 24, 1972, when the provinces of the Philippines were organized into 11 regions by Presidential Decree No. 1 as part of the Integrated Reorganization Plan by President Ferdinand Marcos Sr. The provinces of Aklan, Antique, Capiz, Iloilo (including its then-subprovince of Guimaras), and Negros Occidental were grouped together to form the Western Visayas region.

Overall, Western Visayas is a region rich in natural resources, cultural traditions, and economic potential. Its combination of agricultural strength, growing industries, and a strong tourism sector makes it an important part of the Philippine economy. Despite challenges such as natural disasters and economic inequality, the region continues to develop while preserving its cultural heritage and way of life.
